A permaculture design certificate (PDC) is the foundational training in permaculture, an approach for designing regenerative systems that work with nature.

Over 72+ hours of teaching, you'll learn to observe patterns, understand ecosystems, and design solutions for food, water, energy, shelter, and community. It's internationally recognised and offers both practical tools and a design framework that shifts how you see the world.

Whether you're transforming a balcony, designing a food forest, rethinking your lifestyle, or leading community projects, the PDC gives you the foundation to start.

who this is for

This course is for people who want to understand how to live and design in closer alignment with natural systems, whether or not you have a garden or land.

it's especially for:

  • people without a garden who want practical skills and systems thinking for everyday life, work, and community
  • those considering stewarding land, joining or starting a community, or leading regenerative projects
  • people already living on land or in community who want stronger design foundations
  • activists, facilitators, organizers, educators seeking holistic approaches to social and ecological change
  • researchers, designers, knowledge workers bridging theory with practice
  • anyone feeling the tension between digital life and a longing for deeper connection, responsibility, and ecological belonging

No prior permaculture experience is required. What matters is a willingness to observe, engage, and co-create, both individually and collectively.

002 / lead facilitator

Tom Henfrey

Tom is a certified permaculture diploma tutor, an experienced educator with 20 years of experience in permaculture and ecology. He bridges permaculture, research, and core shamanism, weaving these into his teaching to strengthen both the social and ecological impact of projects.

Tom's background includes:

  • — doctoral research on indigenous environmental knowledge
  • — co-founder of the ECOLISE network for community-led sustainability initiatives in Europe
  • — fellow & management board member at the Schumacher Institute, Bristol, UK
  • — international consultancy and teaching on community-scale ecological and social regeneration

In 2022, he co-founded Quinta Sol na Eira, a permaculture learning centre near Tomar in Central Portugal. Tom is also a practitioner and teacher of core shamanism.

Under Tom's guidance, you'll receive a globally recognised PDC certificate and a foundation to progress toward the Diploma in Applied Permaculture Design.
